chuddai uddi

Chudiya Uddi" is a traditional hand-clapping game played by children in India. The game involves a group of children sitting or standing in a circle with their hands out, palms facing up. One person, usually the leader or "caller," starts the game by saying "Chudiya Uddi" (which translates to "Bangles flew") and raising their hands. The rest of the children mimic the action if the word "Uddi" (flew) is mentioned in context to something that flies. If the caller says something that doesn't fly but raises their hands, anyone who mimics the action is out. The goal is to pay attention and only raise hands for things that can fly.

Guti billa(Tip Cat)

Tipcat, also known as gilli-danda or tip-cat, is a traditional game played in various parts of the world, particularly in India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and the UK. It is an outdoor game that is similar to cricket and baseball but uses different equipment and rules.

Tyre and Stick

The "tyre and stick" game is a traditional children's game played in many parts of the world, especially in rural areas. It involves using a simple stick to roll or guide a tyre (usually an old bicycle or car tyre) along the ground. The game is known by different names in various regions and has been a popular pastime for children for generations due to its simplicity and the minimal equipment required.

Desi Cricket

Desi cricket, often referred to as gully cricket, is a popular informal version of cricket played in the streets, parks, and open spaces in South Asian countries like India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka. It embodies the spirit and love for the game, showcasing how cricket has become an integral part of daily life in these regions.

Cartoon

Children in South Asia, referred to as "desi children," enjoy a wide variety of cartoons, blending local and international content. These cartoons not only entertain but also play a significant role in shaping their cultural and educational experiences.
